Southwest Region

Virginia has had a declining enrollment trend for more than a decade and its no surprise that the southwestern region of Virgnia has had the most decline. The graph below shows that the enrollment has declined more than 10 percent in the majority of western counties.With the declining trend in students it should come to no surprise that the rural western region has experienced a sharp increase in psychiatric crises, overdoes and suicide attempts which is especially devestating in the rural areas. Due to a lack of funding and health care services in the rural Southwestern Virginia a lot of adults and children’s needs were left unattended.

The 2022 County Health Rankings report showed the cities of Bristol, Norton, Buchanan,Dickenson, Lee, Russel, Smuth , Tazewell and Wise are ranked among the bottom 25% of Virginia’s 133 counties. All of these countries were located in western Virginia and were based on health outcomes and other risk factors such as food insecurity, accessibility to services and substance use. In this region the average number of residents per mental health provider is 1,083, and the county with the highest rate is Buchanan County with 3796 residents per one mental health provider. This is well above the average VA rate of 730 residents per one mh provider.
